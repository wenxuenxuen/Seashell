Fun facts about Seashells
Shells are homes for mollusks, but not all mollusks use a shell.
The largest seashell is the Australian Trumpet. It can weigh up to 40 lbs., and are created by gigantic sea snails.
You can guess the age of a seashell. If you can count the number of ridges on the seashell, you can tell how old the shell might be.
Shells that open to the left are very rare. In fact, according to Mental Floss, 9 out 10 shells open to the right.
Never venture and risk a seashell for a pearl; conversely, one can choose and select it in an open field, without risk.
-Wise Old Man
